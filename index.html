WARANGAL & HANAMKONDA—

Warangal Fort is located in Warangal District, Telangana, India. It was the capital city of Kakatiya dynasty and Musunuri Nayakas.
Bhadrakali Temple is one of the oldest temples for Goddess Bhadrakali, situated on a hilltop between the two cities of Hanamkonda and Warangal.
Bhadrakali Bund reflects architectural elegance of the Kakatiya-era - gateways, shelters, sculptural focal points etc. The old town gateways are being used as entrance portals.
Kakatiya zoological park is located on a 50-acre area, and contains a variety of species of birds, animals and reptiles.
The Thousand Pillar Temple or Rudreswara Swamy Temple is a historic Hindu temple located in the town of Hanamakonda, Telangana State, India.It is dedicated to Lord Shiva, Vishnu and Surya.

MULUGU—

Ramappa Temple, also known as the Rudreshwara temple, is a UNESCO World Heritage Site located in Telangana, India.
A magnificent waterfall in the district of Khammam and second biggest waterfall in the State, Bogatha waterfall presents a splendid spectacle of falling waters and rich landscape
Tadvai is a tribal village abutting NH 163 and is located about 76km from Bhupalpally. This is known for its Wildlife Sanctuary.
Lakhnavaram Lake, located in Govindaraopet Mandal about 70 kilometers away from Warangal, is a trending picnic spot. The lake is an exceptional thing of beauty. The lake was formed by closing down three narrow valleys.

HYDERABAD—

The Charminar ('four minarets') constructed in 1591, is a monument located in Hyderabad, Telangana, India. The landmark has become known globally as a symbol of Hyderabad
Golconda Fort (also known as Golla konda (Telugu: "shepherds' hill"), (Urdu: “round hill”), is a fortified citadel built by the Qutb Shahi dynasty
The Salar Jung Museum is an art museum located at Dar-ul-Shifa, on the southern bank of the Musi River in the city of Hyderabad, Telangana, India. It is one of the three National Museums of India
Chowmahalla Palace or Chowmahallat is the palace of the Nizams of Hyderabad State in Hyderabad
Hussain Sagar or Tank-Bund is a heart-shaped lake in Hyderabad, Telangana, built by Ibrahim Quli Qutb Shah in 1563.
Ramoji Film City is an integrated film studio complex located in Hyderabad, India. It is the largest integrated film city in the world
Birla Mandir is a Hindu temple, built on a high hillock called Naubath Pahad on a 13 acres plot in Hyderabad, Telangana, India. The construction took 10 years and was opened in 1976 by Swami Ranganathananda of Ramakrishna Mission
Nehru Zoological Park (also known as Hyderabad Zoo or Zoo Park) is a zoo located near Mir Alam Tank in Hyderabad, Telangana, India. It is one of the most visited destinations in Hyderabad

KHAMMAM—

Khammam Fort is a fort in the city of Khammam, Telangana, India constructed by Musunuri Nayaks
Lakaram lake is a lake in the city of Khammam.The lake is popular for boating.
Kinnerasani Wildlife Sanctuary is a forest located in Bhadradri Kothagudem district, Telangana state of India. Kinnerasani Lake with densely forested islands in the middle of the sanctuary

ADILABAD—

Gnana Saraswati Temple is a Hindu temple of Goddess Saraswati located on the banks of Godavari River at Basar, Telangana, India.
Kuntala Waterfall is a waterfall in the Indian state of Telangana, located on Kadam river in Neradigonda mandal of Adilabad district. It is the highest waterfall in the state with a height of 50 meters.
kadam dam is located across Kadam River, a tributary of Godavari River which enters Telangana from Maharashtra. The dam is located at the confluence of Kadam River into Godavari.

KARIMNAGAR—

Lower Manair Dam also known as LMD was constructed across the Manair River, at Alugunur village, Thimmapur mandal, Karimnagar District, in the Indian state of Telangana during 1974 to 1985.
Deer Park is one of most popular visitor put in Karimnagar. Deer Park is situated in ourskirts of Karimnagar and called as Rajeev Gandhi Deer Park.
The Museum showcases stone tools, coins, terracotta figurines, arms and weapons pertaining to the Karimnagar District which were recovered in the excavations conducted at Peddabankur, Dhulikatta and Kotilingala.

NALGONDA—

Bhuvanagiri Fort is a Fort located in Bhuvanagiri, Yadadri Bhuvanagiri District, Telangana, India.
Pachala Someswara Temple is a Saivite Hindu temple located in Panagal of Nalgonda district, Telangana, India. It is a popular pilgrimage site during Maha Sivaratri.
Chaya Someswara Temple, also known as the Chaya Someshvara Swamy Alayam or the Saila-Somesvara temple, is a Saivite Hindu temple located in Panagal, Nalgonda district of Telangana, India

RANGA REDDY—

Shilparamam is an arts and crafts village located in Madhapur, Hyderabad, Telangana, India. The village was conceived with an idea to create an environment for the preservation of traditional crafts.
Chilkur Balaji Temple, popularly known as "Visa Balaji Temple", is an ancient Hindu temple of Lord Balaji on the banks of Osman Sagar in Rangareddy District.
Ananthagiri Temple is located in this forested area. It is the Birthplace of Musi river, also called as Muchkunda River, which flows through Hyderabad, 5 km from Vikarabad.
Mount Opera Theme park is one of the popular tourist places of Ranga Reddy offering loads of entertainment and fun to each and everyone.
Ocean Park is an amusement park that promises each and every one a day full of entertainment and fun.
Mrugavani National Park is a national park located in Telangana State, India. It is home to a 600 different types of plant life
